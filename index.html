About Shree Chennakeshava Temple...

Shree Chennakeshava Temple situated at Nagaramutt of Hosala Village, Barkur, Brahmavara Taluk, Udupi District is the present witness of a temple which flourished in the 12th century with the aid of Kadamba, Hoysala, Vijayanagar, Ikkeri & Keladi Kings.

This temple hosts a beautiful, smiling, impressive and pleasing structure of Shree Chennakeshava Idol with four hands comprising of Shanka and Padma in the right side of two hands, Chakra and Gadha in the left side of two hands. After the years passed, those so-called kings were perished from the stage along with aids to this temple. Then this temple was isolated from financial aid and after the years this temple survived by the Hebbars family of Nagaramutt and managed by them without any aid whatsoever. This temple became the deity of Nagaramutt and nearby villages.

At a later stage, this temple became dilapidated with a tiled roof and damaged structure of the building with compound walls due to time immemorial. As there was no income of its own or financial help from the government, still daily poojas and other celebrations were performed by the villagers and outside devotees. When years passed, it was difficult to maintain the temple structure and at last it was very difficult to survive the beautiful idol of Shree Chennakeshava.

In this situation, all devotees of Nagaramutt and outside devotees came together to form a committee to rebuild the structure of the temple with Mukha Mantapa etc. In the year 1984, the committee was set up and started working for temple construction with the help of so-called devotees. In the year 1985, May 2nd, the temple was ready according to Agama Shastras, and Prathishta of Shree Chennakeshava was performed with pomp and glory with other rituals and made an arrangement for Nitya Pooja.
